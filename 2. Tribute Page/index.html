Alan Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

Timeline of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London, England.
  • 1936 - Published "On Computable Numbers," which introduced the concept of the Turing machine, laying the foundation for modern computers.
  • 1939 - Joined the Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park to help crack the German Enigma code.
  • 1941 - Turing and his team successfully cracked the Enigma machine, an achievement that significantly shortened WWII.
  • 1945 - Awarded the Order of the British Empire (OBE) for his wartime services.
  • 1950 - Published "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," where he proposed the famous "Turing Test" to measure artificial intelligence.
  • 1954 - Passed away in Wilmslow, Cheshire.
  • 2013 - Received a posthumous royal pardon for his 1952 conviction for "gross indecency."
